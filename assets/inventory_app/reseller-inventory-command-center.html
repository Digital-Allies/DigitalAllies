The Blueprint: Core Structure

The foundation of the system allows for clean CSV exports while remaining human-readable. The key is freezing the top row and keeping headers distinct.

Col A

Status & Visuals

The most important column. Drives conditional formatting and "Days on Market" calculations.

Col F

Cost Basis (The Truth)

What you actually paid. Essential for the "Cash Trapped" metric. Log this immediately in the car.

Col J

Net Profit (The Reward)

The auto-calculated result. This is where the gamification happens.

Lazy Formulas

Automatic Net Profit (Col J)

=IF(H2>0, H2 - I2 - F2, "")

Translation: "If there is a Sold Price (H2), subtract Fees (I2) and Cost (F2) from it. Otherwise, keep the cell clean/empty."

The Stale Item Tracker

=IF(A2="üí∞ Sold", "DONE", TODAY() - B2 & " days")

Translation: "If item is Sold, stop counting. If not, count how many days since you bought it."

Pro-Tips & Integrations

üîç Google Lens Integration
Use the Google Lens app on your phone to find "Comps" (comparable sold prices) instantly while sourcing. Tip: Long-press the price you find in Lens, copy the link, and paste it into a "Notes" column in your sheet so you don't have to research twice when you get home.
üåâ The CSV Bridge (eBay/Depop)
Don't ruin your beautiful inventory sheet with platform-specific formatting. Create a Hidden Tab in Google Sheets. Use formulas like =Inventory!C2 to pull data from your main sheet into the exact column order eBay or Depop requires for their bulk upload CSV tools.
